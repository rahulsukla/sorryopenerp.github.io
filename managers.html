We decided to go old school with the discrimination of OpenERP because one adage that will never lose its charm is ‘Old is Gold’. The six figure salaried professionals or in other terms, managers, take the decision on the deployment of a particular ERP software, so here are some hard truths about OpenERP for the managers.

Memory and CPU Leaks

OpenERP is incapable of running large applications as their ORM is ineffective and cannot be deployed for moderate data sets. If you employ OpenERP to handle a large scale operation, you might as well prepare your IT team to work overtime.

OpenERP is like Superman in the presence of Kryptonite, looks ominous, but is completely ineffective.

Thinking of growing your business with OpenERP? Think again

OpenERP can be a roadblock in the growth of your business as it is incompetent of handling large data applications, so if you are dreaming of increasing your business with OpenERP, you can kiss goodbye to that dream. If you still don’t believe us, here is an example, the stock module of OpenERP computes your stock position by summing up all the transactions from the beginning.

Insecure by Design

We generally assign the same password to many of our accounts, unless you are one of those conspiracy theorists, who think that someone is out to get them. OpenERP uses clear text method to store passwords, which makes it very easy to ascertain the password of your other accounts. This makes your critical information vulnerable to attack. Any kid in a school block with a laptop and an internet connection can hack into your account. Are you ready to bear the repercussion of a security attack?

Community Driven Development (My Ass!)

OpenERP boasts of a large community and they are right, but we now understand the reason for having a large community. OpenERP needed over 250 people to knock some sense into them to fix the broken address bug.

OpenERP v7 contacts issue from sorryopenerp

A beautiful wife or a faithful wife?

We don’t know which one was developed first, Prism or OpenERP, but both programs are built for spying, the only difference is the reason. Prism has listed a safe haven (nothing related to Josh and Julianne starring movie) under the patronage of national security while OpenERP, well, we are not clear about their rationale for collecting sensitive information about the clients without their permission. With information being the highest currency in modern days, OpenERP is making the Watergate scandal look miniscule.

Imperfect and shabby coding

OpenERP’s creators claim it to be the most sought ERP software in present times and yet it has underpaid and overworked junior developers building patches and updates. This seems like paying for a Tom Hanks movie and getting to watch a junior artist wallow about his lost dreams and hopes.The sub-standard coding will increase the development costs and will continue to rise.

Not so OpenERP

The idea of Open Source was founded on the freedom to use the software free of charge with supportive licenses. OpenERP opted for the AGPL license, which restricts its commercial use by enterprisers. Now, we understand that the christening of the software was a subterfuge to hide the real intentions of the company. The irony of Open in OpenERP cannot be validated more.

Investment will only buy you more bad services

The quest for wealth has been the downfall of many empires and it seems that OpenERP is pacing towards its end. By charging for migration between two versions and high-cost customisations, it is clear that the creators of OpenERP are only looking to fill their coffers and not provide value. Perhaps, they have forgotten the first rule of business, ‘Customer always comes first’ or they could be blinded by the sight of Benjamins.

Marketing Driven Development

We initially thought it was Moron Driven Development

The leaders of OpenERP stated the company was open to an acquisition and since then their mission has been aligned to the intention of being bought by companies positioned in the leaders quadrant. Their efforts show that they are aimed at driving up the stock value of the company so that they entice a major company for a takeover. The hype around the lunch module is a clear depiction of the moron driven development.

Pay Registration Fee and become a Partner

The partners of OpenERP are appointed following a policy of money submission rather than talent acquisition. Prior contributions to the field do not matter, if you can pay the registration fee, you are a partner. Becoming a partner is as simple as buying a coffee from the counter. If you think you will get a return for your continued investment, then you are grievously mistaken because it is a fool’s errand.

Over 1000+ useless modules

With the success rate of OpenERP implementation diminishing with time, this ERP software is taking its last breath and will suffer the fate of floppy disks. OpenERP is a collection of over 1000 plus useless and unsupportive modules. OpenERP does not have a predictable model of releasing updates making it difficult for customers to plan their next move. With Darwin’s theory coming into play, OpenERP lacks at several stages of its existence and will become a collector’s item soon.



With Surprize    Without Surprize

Beautiful outside, Ugly Inside

Steve Jobs asked the creators of a Mac to redesign the entire motherboard after it was completely developed because it did not look beautiful. OpenERP’s claims to its beauty are only superficial as the code window still shrieks of disorder. Question any developer about customising OpenERP’s code, you will know what we are talking about.

Unique in being wrong

OpenERP is the host to an incorrect method of calculating values utilising floats. It is the only software which uses this system, which might be marketed as a unique factor, but what is the point of being different if you are wrong.

Migration - PITA

The migration process in OpenERP is not as easy as marketed by the creators of the software. Users will be dependant on the editor for the completion of the migration process in addition to the extra charges. For an ERP, which is publicised as an Open Source software, the creators of OpenERP have continuously discarded the notion of open upgrades. (The Vatican defines that as blasphemy)
