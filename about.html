About the campaign:

The answer was already there. You have seen Morpheus explain to Neo why the system (OpenERP) should not be trusted. The Sorry OpenERP campaign was started to shed light on everything that is wrong with OpenERP. We thought that it was high time that someone showed the world the reality behind the facade. At this point we would like to congratulate the marketing minds behind OpenERP for a job well done, it is not easy to hide so many flaws.

Who are we?

We do not have any connection to Edward Snowden, just making sure people don’t get the wrong idea. We are a group of programmers, (you might know us as the geeks) who are passionate about coding and making things easy rather than complicating them and then spending €3 million to cover it up.

Why this campaign?

By the time you read this, you must be aware of the multiple defects in OpenERP. These imperfections could be corrected, but the administrators at OpenERP preferred a different route. They launched a full blown marketing campaign to cover up the bugs instead of fixing the bugs in their software. Now we know what Voltaire exactly meant when he said, “Common sense is not so common.”

Another reason that prompted us to start this campaign was the evaluation matrix, which concluded that OpenERP trumped SAP in every major department. (Even God is not that perfect)

We have witnessed many people fall prey to the false marketing campaign of OpenERP, so we wanted the customers to think for a moment about their requirements and take an informed decision.
